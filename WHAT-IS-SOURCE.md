# What is Source AI?

Source AI is an autonomous AI engine that automates 80%+ of ERP migrations for mid-market companies. Instead of replacing consultancies, Source partners with them — handling the technical execution of migrations (QuickBooks, Xero to NetSuite, Dynamics 365) in under 21 days, while consultants retain the client relationship, scoping, and commercial ownership.

---

## The Problem

Mid-market companies ($10M–$300M revenue) eventually outgrow their starter accounting systems — QuickBooks, Xero, Sage — and need to migrate to modern ERPs like NetSuite or Microsoft Dynamics 365. Today, this migration is painful, expensive, and slow.

### How migrations work today

A typical QuickBooks-to-NetSuite migration follows a 6-step delivery chain:

1. **End Client** identifies the need, issues an RFP, evaluates vendors
2. **Sales & Scoping** — discovery calls, needs analysis, proposal writing, SOW negotiation
3. **Solutions Architecture** — schema analysis, gap assessment, AS-IS to TO-BE mapping, BRD creation
4. **Resource & Team Build** — find offshore/onshore developers, onboard them, ramp up on client systems
5. **Implementation & Config** — data migration, custom development, integrations, UAT, bug fixing
6. **Go-Live & Handover** — cutover planning, validation, training, hypercare, documentation

**Typical timeline:** 4–6+ months
**Typical cost to the client:** $30K–$40K+

The consulting firms doing this work don't make nearly as much as people assume. For every $200/hr billed, roughly $150 gets eaten by marketing/sales costs to win the deal, PM overhead, offshoring, customer success, and implementation chaos. Typical mid-market consultancy margins are only 20–30%. On a $30K migration, the firm might net ~$6K in profit.

Being a mid-market ERP consultant is a grind — winning net-new migration deals means competing in bake-offs with 4–5 other firms, managing post-migration chaos, change orders, and angry customers. Migration margins are thin, and the work is a time sink.

### The real cost breakdown

**NetSuite OneWorld License (base):** ~$20K/year minimum

| Phase | Hours | Cost |

**Initial BRD quote:** ~$15K (50–60 hrs)

**Total consulting cost:** ~$30K
**Average timeline slip:** +2 months beyond original estimate

## How a Source-Powered Migration Works (Step by Step)

### Phase 1: Discovery (Consultant-led, ~1–2 hours)
The consultant runs their normal discovery call with the client. Nothing changes here — the consultant keeps the relationship.

### Phase 2: System Scan (Source AI, ~1 hour)
Source connects to the client's legacy system (e.g., QuickBooks Enterprise) via API and builds a complete instance map — schemas, fields, customizations, data volumes, integrations.

### Phase 3: Analysis & Questionnaire (Source AI, ~2–3 hours)
Source infers business logic and workflows from the scanned data, identifies edge cases, and auto-generates a structured questionnaire for the consultant to deliver to the client.

### Phase 4: BRD Generation (Source AI, ~2 hours)
Source produces a full Business Requirements Document — AS-IS state, TO-BE state, gap analysis, data mapping, and migration plan. The consultant reviews and confirms with the client.

### Phase 5: Configuration & Migration (Source AI, ~8 hours)
Source configures the target system (NetSuite), executes the data migration (ETL), and runs validation checks. Zero human hours on the migration execution itself.

### Phase 6: QA & Validation (Source AI, ~2 hours)
Automated testing, reconciliation, and proof artifacts generated for the consultant to present to the client.

### Phase 7: Go-Live (Consultant + Source AI, ~1 day)
Cutover, final verification, and the consultant handles UAT / training with the client.

**Total: ~18 days end-to-end. ~12 hours of consultant time. Zero offshore developers.**

## The Vision

Source AI is not a tool. It fundamentally changes how consulting works in the age of AI.

Source AI collapses that delivery chain. The consultant becomes the relationship owner and quality controller. The AI does the work. The client gets a faster, cheaper, better migration. Everyone wins.

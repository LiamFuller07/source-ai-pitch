## Slide 1: The Problem — How Migrations Work Today

**Section Label:** THE PROBLEM
**Headline:** How Migrations Work Today

**Message:** ERP migrations are painfully slow, expensive, multi-step delivery chains that frustrate everyone involved.

### The 6-Step Delivery Chain (Today)

| Step | Phase | What Happens |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 01 | **End Client** | Identifies need, issues RFP, evaluates vendors |
| 02 | **Sales & Scoping** | Discovery calls, needs analysis, proposal writing, SOW negotiation |
| 03 | **Solutions Architecture** | Schema analysis, gap assessment, AS-IS → TO-BE mapping, BRD creation |
| 04 | **Resource & Team Build** | Find offshore/onshore devs, onboard, ramp up on client systems |
| 05 | **Implementation & Config** | Data migration, custom development, integrations, UAT, bug fixing |
| 06 | **Go-Live & Handover** | Cutover planning, validation, training, hypercare, documentation |

**Timeline bar:** Month 0 → Month 6+ | **2–6+ months typical**

### What we're getting across:
- The current process is bloated — 6 handoffs, multiple teams, months of work
- Each step cascades into the next, creating a fragile chain
- The staircase visual shows increasing complexity and cost over time
- The timeline bar drives home that this isn't days or weeks — it's months

## Appendix: Sales Call Reference Transcript

> Liam Fuller (Source AI CEO) pitching to an Australian ERP consultancy partner prospect. The prospect has PwC/Deloitte background and runs a NetSuite/ERP consultancy focused on private mid-market companies across Australia.

### Prospect Background

- Former PwC and Deloitte — built out ERP advisory offerings at both firms
- Started own practice in 2020 focused on private market companies outgrowing legacy software
- Works with clients across Australia and internationally
- Focus: growing companies moving from legacy systems to fit-for-purpose ERP
- Typical clients: wholesale distribution with WMS, manufacturing, global operations (NetSuite OneWorld), not just core finance
- Legacy systems common in Australia: **Xero**, **MYOB** (Account Right / EXO), bespoke built solutions

### Key Discussion Points

#### AI's Impact on ERP (Prospect's View)

- AI has been a "real game changer" in the last 12–18 months in two ways:
  1. **Consulting delivery** — work that used to be manual is now much more streamlined
  2. **ERP vendor innovation** — rapid developments, especially around MCP (Model Context Protocol) connectors
- MCP is seen as potentially transformative — companies can bring their own agent and connect directly to the ERP database
- Different vendors taking different approaches to AI:
  - **Dynamics** — pushing MCP "crazy hard," impressive demos
  - **NetSuite** — lots of products but timelines feel slow ("end of 2026" feels too late)
  - Some vendors trying to build AI into the platform with "varying degrees of success" and likely technical debt
- Prospect believes the MCP approach will "shape the narrative for the next 12–18 months"
- Traditional SaaS licensing models "are going to be challenged in a pretty significant way"
- Some functional areas still need traditional interfaces (e.g., warehousing/WMS), but finance, reporting, sales, customer service — "up for some significant change"
- Big question: "Where are these ERP systems going to find their value if someone can spin up an ERP system for themselves in two years?"

#### Liam's Pitch Walkthrough

- Walked through the deck live, covering:
  - The problem (6-step delivery chain, 2–6+ months)
  - Source AI as an engine (not a tool) that automates 80% of the migration work
  - Before/after delivery chain — Source replaces Solutions Architect + Offshoring
  - Consultant keeps the relationship, Source does the technical execution
  - System scan → fixed price offering for consultants
  - BRD generation, implementation, configuration, data mapping — all autonomous
  - Sub-21-day migrations achieved on first migration
  - Wild Tech case study: €11K fixed price, 13-day delivery
  - Supported systems: QuickBooks → NetSuite today, Dynamics/Business Central just completed, expanding to other legacy systems and on-prem
  - Integration support: Shopify, HubSpot, Stripe attached to core migrations
  - Partnership model: increase margins, lower price to win deals, white label delivery
  - First migration free, revenue share model

#### Live Demo Walkthrough

- Showed the backend infrastructure (not consultant-facing)
- Demo flow: uploaded discovery call notes + transcript, gave Source read access to a QuickBooks environment and a NetSuite environment
- Source performs a system scan, then builds a mini-BRD UI for the next discovery call
- Demo observations from the live scan:
  - QuickBooks environment had $33M+ balance (intentionally complex test instance)
  - Source identified deferred revenue, opening balance issues, duplicate vendors
  - Found AP/AR breakdowns
  - Discovered **physical products miscategorized** — all revenue from Shopify connector flowing through a discount account instead of proper sales revenue ("revenue reported in QuickBooks is fundamentally misstated at the gross revenue line")
  - Generated: executive summary, overall data readiness assessment, account counts, vendor/customer totals, critical findings
  - Company profile built from conversation + scan: channel mix, e-commerce/retail/wholesale, sourcing & supply chain
  - Key stakeholders identified from meeting notes
  - Month-end close analysis with "what the client stated" vs. system reality
  - Migration strategy and phasing recommendations
  - Data mapping with migration decisions (e.g., "do not migrate, build from Shopify")
  - Auto-generated questions for next discovery call with impact assessments and responsible stakeholders
  - Option A / Option B recommendations with Source's default recommendation

#### Prospect's Complexity Question

- Asked about typical client profile — core finance only, or more complex?
- Liam clarified: Source handles complexity in terms of **data depth** (AP, AR, integrations, workflows attached to one central system), not yet multiple disparate systems
- "When we talk about complexity it will be about the number of systems, it won't actually be about the amount of data or the complexity of the data"

#### Competitive Landscape

- Prospect mentioned **Trove** as a potentially similar offering they'd recently come across
- Liam's differentiation: Trove goes **direct to end clients** (trying to remove consultants entirely), Source **partners with consultancies** (consultants keep the relationship)
- Liam's view: "AI is a little bit far from" replacing the human consultant entirely — clients want a human relationship for costly, high-stakes ERP migrations

#### Australian Market Insight

- Legacy systems in Australia: **MYOB** (Account Right / EXO) owns a significant share of the legacy market, plus **Xero**
- Xero was "not too hard" compared to QuickBooks for Source to handle
- MYOB is a target for Source to support
- NetSuite Suite Connect conference coming up in Sydney in next few weeks — AI positioning will be the keynote headline
- Prospect knows the Wild Tech team in Australia

#### Outcome

- Prospect requested follow-up materials to "digest further with the team"
- Positive reception: "That is very cool" / "Thanks again for your time"
- Prospect wants details sent through post-call
